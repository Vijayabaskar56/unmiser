unmiser

A money tracker that reads your bank SMS on-device and quietly bends your spending habits. The look is borrowed from Minna Bank: editorial black & white, sharp hairline cards, charming line drawings — disciplined enough to make numbers calm.

00

Foundations

Five principles the whole system answers to. When a decision is unclear, these break the tie.

1

Numbers are the hero

Balances and totals are set in big, tight, tabular weights. Everything else recedes to support them. A screen should answer one money question at a glance.

2

Ink, not color

The interface is black on white. Color carries meaning only — yellow marks the one thing that matters on a screen; green means money came in. Never decorate.

3

Hairlines & sharp edges

Structure comes from thin 1–1.5px rules and near-square 3px corners, not shadows or fills. The result feels like fine print — precise, trustworthy.

4

Drawings, not icons

Money types get warm single-weight line illustrations (a wallet, a piggy bank). They humanise an otherwise austere, data-dense surface.

5

Tags over taxonomy

Spending is organised by lightweight #hashtags, not a rigid category tree. Flexible, personal, and fast to assign from a parsed SMS.

Use yellow for a single focal point per screen — the live month, the urgent nudge, the chart's current bar.

Don't

Fill cards or backgrounds with yellow, or use green for anything other than incoming money.

02

Accent

Yellow is the default, but the accent is the one thing the user can personalise — changed in Settings → Appearance. It recolors every active state, chart marker, highlight and key action in a single move; everything else stays ink. Text on the accent is always ink (--on-hl), and the accent is never used for surfaces or large fills. Tap a swatch to preview the whole system live.

Illustration

Single-weight (2px) black line drawings on white, sharp-cornered frame. One per money type. They carry the brand's warmth — keep them simple, slightly playful, never shaded or filled.

12

Voice & tone

unmiser talks like a sharp, kind friend who's good with money — plain, specific, never preachy. It states the number, then offers one action.

We say

"Food delivery is up 40% this week."

Specific, observed, leads to one action. Numbers in plain words.

We don't say

"You're overspending! Be careful! 😰"

No shame, no alarms, no emoji clutter, no vague scolding.
